A DCF values a business as the sum of all the cash flows it will generate, discounted to the PRESENT VALUE at a RATE that reflects the riskiness of the cashflows.


We define cash flows as the operating cash flows - cash reinvestment which makes sense. 

The discount rate is the required rate of return for the investors and is a function of the riskiness of the cash flows.

The comparable analysis may give a lower market value in comparison to the DCF.

This is because the market may value the underlying security lower than what is currently priced in.

Or we can inflate our model.

There are two approaches to a discounted cash flow.

1. unlevered DCF means your are trying to find the value of the operations to all providers of capital
	This calculation gives you the enterprise value
	Cash flows are higher than levered approach


2.   levered approach means you are trying to find the value of the business to equity owners.
	These calculations give you the equity value.
	Discount rate = cost of equity 
	Cash flows are lower than the unlevered approach
	Note: used for banks
